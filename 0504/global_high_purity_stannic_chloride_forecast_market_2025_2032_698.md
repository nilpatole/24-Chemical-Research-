Definition</p><p>
</p><p>High Purity Stannic Chloride, also known as tin(IV) chloride, is a chemical compound with the formula SnCl4. It is a colorless, hygroscopic liquid that is used as a precursor to other tin compounds. High Purity Stannic Chloride is employed in various industrial processes, including the production of tin plating, surface treatment of metals, and as a catalyst in organic synthesis. Its high purity form is essential for applications where the presence of impurities could have detrimental effects on the final product.</p><p>
