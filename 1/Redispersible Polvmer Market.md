Redispersible polymer powder (RPP) is a specialized polymer that, when mixed with water, can redisperse to form aqueous polymer dispersions. Upon curing, these dispersions form a continuous polymer film. This film provides several benefits to dry building mixtures, such as enhanced water-holding capacity, improved adhesion, and better workability. These properties make RPP an essential component in construction products like adhesives, tile grout, and renders.

In essence, redispersible polymer powders are widely used in construction and building applications due to their ability to provide superior bonding and flexibility in dry mixes. The powder is created through a drying process, which preserves the polymer's ability to be easily re-dispersed upon contact with water, making it highly versatile and effective in enhancing construction materials.
